What can I do?

One of the most important things that you and others can do to prevent gentrification is to be educated on how to be part of the solution and not the problem. You can begin to learn about factors that negatively impact individuals and small businesses in gentrified communities. A lack of rent control is a significant concern in many historically disadvantaged communities. As rent inflates, many people have no choice but to relocate to more impoverished places. Whilst local companies often have to close their shops or transfer away from their homes to keep their businesses open. As long-term residents depart and small enterprises leave, popular corporations and higher-income people start to settle into these neighborhoods. To help maintain the landmarks of our communities, we can begin helping displaced individuals and local-businesses and mom-and-pop shops instead of large corporations. If we continue to educate ourselves and others on the consequences of gentrification, we will become part of the solution.

Supporting Local Businesses

A simple act of purchasing at a local company is a way to show our appreciation for what they do for our community. In addition, as a community, we can also help the owners raise payment to their workers, pay their energy and light bills, renovate their businesses, and provide food to their families. By supporting local businesses, we are supporting the businesses that contribute to the distinctiveness of our neighborhoods.

Misconception: Gentrification is not Revitalization

Revitalization is driven by and for citizens with assistance from the public and private sectors to improve their neighborhoods.

    Some of the improvements include:
  • Safer and cleaner neighborhoods
  • Improving housing

    • Gentrification is when homes are no longer affordable for low-income people. Meanwhile, rejuvenation is when residents have the availability to remain in their neighborhoods.
